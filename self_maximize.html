Height

First Impressions

Our monkey brain likes to percieve taller people as stronger and more confident. Similar to when two lobsters meet for the first time, we want to see where we stand on the totem pole before acting accordingly. Height is a simple and straightforward indicator that we use to see if we should start off our relationship from a docile or assertive standpoint. When stepping to a taller person, we practice caution, looking up at them, our subconscious mind is telling us that this bigger creature in front of us might have more power. We might need to tread carefully, so that power is not used against us. On the other hand, looking down at a shorter person, we physically stand over their position. We feel safe enough to be more assertive, maybe even trying to confirm our dominance over them.

Continued relationships

Height loses its importance somewhat as we get to know a person. Their other qualities, that need more time to discover, come through. We might have realized that even if the person is shorter than us, they assume a leading role in the relationship/group via their other qualities like confidence and perseverance. Still, when a new taller person approaches, our monkey brain still engages the same thought: "This person seems taller than the person I know, is he also more X in this Y quality?"

⚡ Self-maximize?

Stand tall, with your shoulders back. Because height is a part of the size bias, work on becoming more muscular and/or fit.

If shorter, standing closer to a taller person will force you to tilt your head back more. This will emphasize your shortness. This can be used to great advantage when you want to inspire confidence in the other person.

If taller, stand closer to a shorter person if you would like to be more assertive. Be careful not to cast a shadow on someone you don't want to overshadow.
